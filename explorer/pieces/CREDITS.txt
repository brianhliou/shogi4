Piece artwork: Oca Studios — Shogi4 ("Four" series), released into the PUBLIC DOMAIN.
Source: ocastudios.com/four/shogi · BGG boardgame/146291/shogi4

These are the official piece tiles, extracted from the public-domain Android app
com.ocastudios.shogi4 v1.0.1 (the same app whose logic.py was decompiled to recover
the ruleset — see research/prior-art-evidence/). Each tile already carries the
authentic movement marks (paw prints, in the piece's forward sense); base pieces sit
on a cream card and evolved pieces on a pink card.

  crane.png      King — Crane (first player's royal)
  pheasant.png   King — Pheasant (second player's royal)
  carp.png       Carp        -> koi.png      Koi      (evolved)
  tapir.png      Tapir       -> baku.png     Baku     (evolved)
  raccoon.png    Raccoon-dog -> tanuki.png   Tanuki   (evolved)
  fox.png        Fox         -> kitsune.png  Kitsune  (evolved)

The sprites here are used exactly as shipped (pristine). The viewer rotates the second
player's tiles 180° (as in the app) so each tile's painted move-marks point forward for
that side. The variants in dark/ are auto-generated (tools/gen_dark_cards.py) for the
second player's non-royal pieces: only the card background is darkened ~12% by a border
flood-fill; the animal and the paw move-marks are left at full colour.

No attribution is legally required (public domain); credit to Oca Studios is given
out of courtesy in the README and the "How it works" panel.
